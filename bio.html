My progress thus far

My first piece of code was written in 2010, at the time not knowing where it would lead. My first experiences using code included making my own simple "choose your own adventure" games and building small programs in Python to obtain my math homework solutions. I was soon making small 2-D games, exercising my creativity with code, and having a lot of fun. I participated in several afterschool programming events and loved every second of challenge and delayed gratification I was able to enjoy.
However, I did not stick to programming as, even though I greatly enjoyed programming, my interests shifted elsewhere. After highschool I enrolled in college, imagining myself as an artist. The path of becoming an artist was fraught with struggle and long periods of dissatisfaction. It was at this point that I decided I wanted to do something more realistic.

In February, 2017, I started the Front End Development course offered by Free Code Camp. I quickly learned HTML, jQuery, CSS, and the fundamentals of Javascript. It was certainly a struggle, but I felt my love of programming returning to me. From there, I learned the basics of Express, MongoDB, and Node with help from Free Code Camp and several courses on Udemy. Currently, I'm working on finishing my first project, TwitchWatch. It's a full stack project employing MongoDB, Express, React, and Node. You may visit my finished projects in the gallery section of my portfolio.
